Director, UX Research

San Francisco, CA

About This Role Strava is the app for active people. With over 150 million athletes in more than 185 countries, it’s more than tracking workouts—it’s where connection, motivation, and personal bests thrive. No matter your activity, gear, or goals, Strava’s got you covered. Find your crew, crush your milestones, and keep moving forward. Start your journey with Strava today. Our mission is simple: to motivate people to live their best active lives. We believe in the power of movement to connect and drive people forward. As the Director, UX Research you will be responsible for leading the User Experience Research team and driving a deep understanding of user needs, behaviors, and motivations. You will partner with senior leadership and cross-functional partners, driving research that shapes company and product strategy and ensures decisions are informed by data and meaningful insights. You will manage a team of researchers and report directly to the Chief Design Officer. We are looking for a UX Research leader who can establish a vision and culture that empowers a team of talented researchers to deliver exceptional research. You will drive research across multiple user experiences (free and subscriber, novice to elite athletes, focused on running and cycling). You will drive the creation of research roadmaps in partnership with cross-functional stakeholders and determine the priorities for your team. The right candidate will coach their team from a deep understanding of UX research best practices, employ a variety of methodologies and embody a nimble, creative, and pragmatic approach that quickly unlocks new insights that have tangible impact to the product and business outcomes. We follow a flexible hybrid model that generally translates to around half your time on-site in our San Francisco office —roughly three days per week. What You’ll Do: You think big and spearhead complex research initiatives that have a tangible impact on company and product strategy, user satisfaction and business outcomes. You lead high-visibility research projects, collaborating closely with key stakeholders such as Design, Product Management, Data Science, Analytics, and executives. You are a recognized expert within the organization utilizing your knowledge and experience to train and educate stakeholders on best practices. You champion and lead with deep empathy for our customers by seamlessly integrating insights from diverse sources, including primary and secondary research, industry news, and operational data. You lead the objective-setting and discovery process, including user persona development, critical user journey-mapping, and qualitative and quantitative methods. You are a role model, mentor, and motivator for your team. You are invested in developing talent and creating an inspiring environment and team culture. You forge high-trust relationships with colleagues, fostering collaboration and innovation and by doing so, you influence teams to act on research insights by providing clear, actionable recommendations and relevant business context. You develop processes that drive quality and efficiency to scale and deliver consistent, high quality research. You embrace diversity of perspective yet have a strong point of view that can cut through the clutter. You Will Be Successful Here Because You Can: Plan, Define and Implement our Research Roadmap: Define and create a quarterly and annual roadmap that aligns with our product roadmap and business priorities. Manage research plans, timelines, and budgets to ensure projects are completed on schedule. Conduct and Direct User Research: Plan and execute qualitative and quantitative research studies across a wide variety of needs: large scale surveys, usability, trust and safety, monetization, B2B, etc. Prepare detailed reports, personas, journey maps, and presentations to effectively communicate insights to stakeholders. Measure and Analyze Data: Interpret and synthesize research findings to generate actionable insights and recommendations for product improvements. Develop metrics (like but not limited to CSAT, NPS) to measure user satisfaction. Stay Updated: Keep abreast of the latest UX research methods, tools, and industry trends to continuously improve research practices. Conduct Competitive Analysis: Analyze competitors' products to understand market trends and identify opportunities for innovation. Facilitate Workshops: Lead workshops and brainstorming sessions to gather input from stakeholders and users. Oversee research operations: Manage third party agencies, participant recruiting and incentives, focus groups, unmoderated testing, etc. What You’ll Bring to the Team: 8+ years of UX Research experience in a corporate agile setting 2+ years at Director level Bachelor’s degree in Psychology, Social, Behavioral Sciences, or Human-Computer Interaction. Master’s or PhD degree preferred. Expertise in a wide range of qualitative and quantitative research methods, including pathfinding work, usability studies, interviews, focus groups, surveys, card sorts, and heuristic reviews, large quantitative surveys. Data Science experience is a plus. Exceptional communication skills, adept at translating data into actionable insights both verbally and in writing Expertise in data-driven storytelling, transforming raw data into compelling narratives and visualizations Expert in taking user personas and working with Data Science to build customer cohorts Familiarity with quantitative and qualitative research tools like QuestionPro and UserTesting, as well as UX methodologies such as Design Thinking and Lean UX Commitment to ongoing learning and staying current with industry techniques and tools Strong organizational, analytical, critical thinking, and team-building abilities Proven track record of effective collaboration with cross-functional teams Compensation Overview: At Strava, we know our employees are the most important ingredient to our success, and our compensation and total rewards programs reflect that. Account Manager, Strava for Business

San Francisco, CA

About the Role Strava is the app for active people. With over 150 million athletes in more than 185 countries, Strava is where connection, motivation, and personal bests thrive. No matter your activity, gear, or goals, we help you find your crew, crush your milestones, and keep moving forward. Start your journey with Strava today. Our mission is simple: to motivate people to live their best active lives. We believe in the power of movement to connect and drive people forward. Strava for Business partners with brands looking to authentically engage with our highly engaged global community. Through our innovative advertising and challenge-based campaigns, we provide an alternative to traditional marketing channels, allowing brands to inspire and connect with athletes in a meaningful way. As an Account manager, you’ll act as a trusted advisor to partners, helping them achieve their business objectives while demonstrating the long-term value of Strava’s solutions. Your ability to build strong client relationships, identify growth opportunities, and drive renewals will be key to the success of both our partners and Strava. This role requires a combination of client relationship management, campaign strategy, and operations knowledge to ensure our partners achieve success in their fitness and engagement goals. Strava has a positive and encouraging culture - we are looking for inquisitive, humble, hard working and collaborative teammates who are able to balance the execution of challenges, driving upsells while keeping both the partner and Strava user value in mind! We follow a flexible hybrid model that generally translates to around half your time on-site in our San Francisco office—roughly three days per week. You’re excited about this opportunity because you will: Own a portfolio of mid market partners and collaborate closely with internal teams to build seamless campaign experiences and improve client success. Support partners through the entire campaign lifecycle, from contracts all the way to challenge builds & reporting using a variety of tools (including but not limited to Asana, Tableau, SQL, Hubspot). Be responsible for renewals and upsells by using data driven recommendations and demonstrating the impact of Strava’s advertising solutions to secure long term partnerships. Act as the ears and eyes for the product team, gathering and compiling partner and product feedback to help inform product improvements. Stay on the cutting edge of Strava’s platform features and industry trends, applying this knowledge to improve campaigns. You will be successful here by: Being a strong communicator. The ability to set expectations, influence stakeholders, and handle multiple conversations at once is critical. Being proactive, taking initiative to tackle challenges, optimize processes, and identifying new opportunities for partners based on performance data and client feedback. Demonstrating outstanding attention to detail, product knowledge and problem solving skills to ensure a high standard of execution, from strategy to reporting while balancing speed with quality. Embracing change & adapting quickly. Being comfortable with shifting priorities, new product offerings, and a constantly evolving digital landscape is key to success. Staying athlete & community-centric. Strava’s success is rooted in its passionate user base, and we want brands to enhance, rather than disrupt, the athlete experience. We’re excited about you because: You have 5+ years of experience working in an account management or client services role, managing media agencies and/or large brands. You excel in communication and relationship-building, thriving in collaborative environments with internal and external partners. You are proactive, consistently finding ways to enhance customer value, improve operational processes, and drive overall business success.. You are adaptable, able to handle multiple priorities in a fast-paced environment while meeting deadlines and ensuring client satisfaction remains a top priority. You are comfortable working towards achieving revenue goals and use data to inform recommendations and decision-making. You are passionate about fitness, technology and the power of community.
